A solar power plant is a facility that generates electricity by converting sunlight into electrical energy using photovoltaic (PV) cells. These cells, typically made of silicon, absorb sunlight and release electrons, creating a direct current (DC) electricity. This DC electricity is then converted into alternating current (AC) electricity, which can be used to power 1 homes, businesses, and the electrical grid.
